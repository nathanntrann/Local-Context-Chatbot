# Thermal Seal Inspection

Thermal seal inspection uses infrared imaging to evaluate the quality of heat-sealed packaging. The system captures thermal images of seals as products move along a conveyor and classifies them as PASS (acceptable) or FAULT (defective).

## How It Works

1. **Heat Application**: A sealing bar applies heat and pressure to create a seal
2. **Thermal Imaging**: An IR camera captures the thermal signature immediately after sealing
3. **Analysis**: Software analyzes the thermal pattern to detect defects
4. **Classification**: Each seal is classified as PASS or FAULT based on configured thresholds

## What Makes a Good Seal

A properly formed thermal seal shows:
- **Uniform temperature distribution** across the seal area
- **Consistent width** along the entire seal length
- **No cold spots** that indicate incomplete bonding
- **No hot spots** that indicate material burn-through
- **Clean edges** without wrinkles or folds

## Common Defect Types

- **Cold seal**: Insufficient heat — seal area appears cooler than expected
- **Partial seal**: Only part of the seal formed correctly
- **Wrinkled seal**: Material folded during sealing, creating uneven thermal patterns
- **Burn-through**: Excessive heat damaged the material
- **Contamination**: Foreign material in the seal area disrupted bonding
